Photosynthesis Notes

Complete Biology Notes by StudyVerse

Introduction

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae and some bacteria prepare their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide and water. During this process, light energy is converted into chemical energy stored in the form of glucose. Oxygen is released into the atmosphere as a by-product, making photosynthesis essential for life on Earth.

Definition

Photosynthesis is a biological process in which green plants, algae and some bacteria use sunlight to convert carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O) into glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆). Oxygen (O₂) is released as a by-product. This process is essential because it provides food for plants and produces oxygen needed by most living organisms.

Chemical Equation of Photosynthesis

Reactants Products
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Sunlight C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Requirements for Photosynthesis

  • Sunlight
  • Chlorophyll
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)
  • Water (H₂O)
  • Healthy Green Leaves

Where Does Photosynthesis Take Place?

Photosynthesis mainly occurs in the leaves of green plants. Inside the leaf are tiny structures called chloroplasts, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll. Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and uses that energy to produce glucose from carbon dioxide and water.

Steps of Photosynthesis

  1. Absorption of Sunlight: Chlorophyll captures light energy from the Sun.
  2. Absorption of Water: Roots absorb water from the soil and transport it to the leaves.
  3. Intake of Carbon Dioxide: Carbon dioxide enters the leaves through tiny pores called stomata.
  4. Formation of Glucose: The absorbed light energy is used to convert water and carbon dioxide into glucose.
  5. Release of Oxygen: Oxygen is released into the atmosphere through the stomata.

Importance of Photosynthesis

  • Produces food for plants.
  • Releases oxygen into the atmosphere.
  • Removes carbon dioxide from the air.
  • Maintains the balance of gases in nature.
  • Supports almost every food chain on Earth.
  • Stores solar energy as chemical energy.

Factors Affecting Photosynthesis

Factor Effect
Sunlight More sunlight increases the rate of photosynthesis up to an optimum level.
Carbon Dioxide Higher CO₂ concentration generally increases the rate of photosynthesis.
Water Water is an essential raw material. A shortage reduces photosynthesis.
Temperature Photosynthesis works best within an optimum temperature range. Very high or very low temperatures reduce the rate.
Chlorophyll Healthy green leaves with sufficient chlorophyll perform photosynthesis more efficiently.

Applications of Photosynthesis

  • Provides food for plants and supports the entire food chain.
  • Produces oxygen required by humans and animals.
  • Helps reduce carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.
  • Maintains ecological balance.
  • Stores solar energy in the form of glucose.

Summary

Photosynthesis is one of the most important biological processes on Earth. It enables plants to prepare their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide and water. The process also releases oxygen, making life possible for most living organisms and helping maintain the balance of gases in the atmosphere.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make food using sunlight, carbon dioxide and water.

2. Where does photosynthesis occur?

It mainly occurs in the chloroplasts of green plant leaves.

3. Why is chlorophyll important?

Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and provides the energy needed for photosynthesis.

4. What are the raw materials for photosynthesis?

The main raw materials are carbon dioxide and water. Sunlight provides the energy for the process.

5. What is the main product of photosynthesis?

Glucose is the main food produced, and oxygen is released as a by-product.
